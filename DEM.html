Digital Elevation Models

Digital elevation modelsstrong> contain elevation information; where each cell contains a real value representing elevation. Modern DEMs are often derived from Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) data, which captures high-resolution spatial information by sending out LASER pulses and calculating their return times.

Digital Terrain Models (DTM):provide elevation data from the bare ground only, effectively removing features like trees and buildings.

Digital Surface Models (DSM):These include all features on the Earth's surface, such as the tops of trees, buildings, and other structures.

I mapped the area between the Zuid-Holland / Noord-Brabant border called the National Park De Biesbosch. Particularly since I had heard of the place through Dutch friends, and the waterways looked interesting to me. I wanted to see how much they changed over time.

I found a map from 1869 titled (translated) “General plan of the Bergsche Veld with indication of the Nieuwe Merwede and the works carried out to form it up to the end of 1869”. This map was commissioned by the State, established by Royal Decree of 13 February 1869, secretary of Opstal.

I learned:

  • Manipulating DSM/DTM maps
  • Correlating two different maps and coordinating them together
  • Clipping into a polygram
  • Exporting with full layout

This style of mappin reveals the land reliefs clearly to the viewer. Despite being 2D, it gives a color value to different elevations which allow to see anomalies in land and different man-made structures clearly. Even by the shift in colors surrounding waterways, it allows to see younger riverbeds and transformed ones.

Terrain information of this kind is essential for assesments for building infrastructure such as managing waterways through dams or subterrain buildings like tunnels.
